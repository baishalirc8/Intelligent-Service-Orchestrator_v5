HOLOCRON AI User Manual

Your step-by-step guide to mastering the Automation Orchestration Platform

Welcome to HOLOCRON AI

HOLOCRON AI is your intelligent partner for managing IT operations. Think of it as a digital command center where you can:

  • Organize your IT team into departments ("Crews") with clearly defined roles ("Agents")
  • Assign AI assistants to work alongside your human team members, handling routine tasks automatically
  • Monitor your infrastructure in real-time with proactive alerting and AI-driven remediation
  • Manage IT operations with ITIL-aligned incident, problem, change, and service request workflows
  • Orchestrate work through approval-gated pipelines, task management, and knowledge sharing
Tip: If this is your first time, the platform will walk you through a guided tour after you log in. You can restart the tour at any time from your profile menu.

First Login & Onboarding

When you first access HOLOCRON AI, you'll go through a quick setup process:

  1. Create your account — Enter your username, password, and display name on the registration page. If you already have an account, simply log in.
  2. Enter company information — Tell us your company name, industry, and size. This helps us recommend the best AI agents for your organization.
  3. Choose your departments — Select which Crews (departments) are relevant to your organization. You can always add more later.
  4. Review AI recommendations — HOLOCRON AI suggests optimal agent assignments based on your company profile. You can accept, modify, or skip these recommendations.
  5. Start using the platform — You're taken to the Crews & Agents page where you can see your organizational chart and begin customizing.
Tip: Look for the Setup Progress indicator in the top-right corner of the header. It shows you what steps are left to fully configure your platform.

Setting Up Your Crews

Crews are your departments. The platform comes pre-loaded with industry-standard IT departments, each containing a full hierarchy of roles.

How to View Your Crews

  1. Navigate to Organization → Crews & Agents from the sidebar
  2. You'll see a visual organizational chart grouped by department
  3. Each card represents an Agent (role) with its title, department, and AI capability status

Available Departments

The platform includes departments such as: Security Operations, Network Operations, Cloud Operations, DevOps, Data Management, Infrastructure, Applications, IT Service Management, Compliance & Risk, End User Computing, and the Executive office.

Managing Agents

Agents are the individual roles within your Crews. Each agent has defined responsibilities and AI capabilities.

Subscribing to an Agent

  1. Go to Crews & Agents and find the role you want to activate
  2. Click on the role card to view its details — responsibilities, AI capabilities, and cost
  3. Click Subscribe to add the agent to your active roster
  4. The agent will appear in your Active Agents list

Active Agents Page

Navigate to Organization → Active Agents to see all your subscribed agents. From here you can:

  • View each agent's current status and assignments
  • Enable or disable the AI shadow for any agent
  • Remove agents you no longer need

Enabling AI Agents

Each role subscription has an AI Shadow toggle. When you enable it, the platform assigns an AI agent to that role. The AI agent will:

  • Monitor infrastructure assets assigned to its role
  • Generate alerts when it detects anomalies or threshold breaches
  • Propose remediation actions for critical issues
  • Build best-practice metric profiles based on the role's responsibilities
  • Provide insights through the Agent Chat interface
Important: AI agents augment your human team — they don't replace them. All critical remediation actions require human approval before execution. You stay in control.

AI Recommendations

Using the Cockpit

Tip: The Cockpit is the best starting point each day. It gives you an instant overview of your infrastructure health and any issues that need attention.

Deploying Probes

Probes are lightweight agents you install on your network to discover and monitor devices. To set up a probe:

  1. Go to Infrastructure → Configuration → Configure
  2. Click Add Probe and choose a probe type:
    • Coupled — Always connected, real-time data
    • Semi-Autonomous — Works offline, syncs when connected
    • Fully Autonomous — Self-contained, can self-remediate
  3. Configure the probe name, network range, and credentials
  4. Download the probe agent script and install it on your target server
  5. The probe will start heartbeating back to the platform and discovering devices

Adding Credentials

Probes need credentials to access devices via SNMP, SSH, or WMI. Go to the Credentials section in Configure to add them. You can link multiple credentials to a single probe for environments with different authentication requirements.

Probe Clustering

For large environments, you can scale a probe by adding cluster nodes. Open a probe's detail panel and go to the Cluster tab to add nodes with different hardware tiers (Entry, Standard, Performance, Enterprise).

Configuring AI Providers

HOLOCRON AI supports multiple AI backends. To configure your providers:

  1. Go to Infrastructure → Configuration → Service Metrics → AI Providers tab
  2. Click Add Provider and select the type (OpenAI, Google Gemini, Anthropic Claude, or Custom)
  3. Enter your API key and optionally customize the model and base URL
  4. Click Test to verify the connection works
  5. Set a provider as default, or assign specific providers to individual agent roles
Tip: You can assign different AI providers to different agent roles. For example, use a cheaper model for routine monitoring tasks and a more capable model for complex analysis.

Managing AI Costs

HOLOCRON AI includes built-in cost optimization. Navigate to Service Metrics → AI Cost Estimator to see your estimated costs and how caching reduces them.

Cost Optimization Features

  • Template Cache — AI responses are cached by asset type, so similar devices reuse the same analysis. View cache statistics in the Cache Intelligence tab.
  • Rate Limiting — Built-in cooldowns prevent accidental duplicate API calls when clicking buttons rapidly
  • KB-First Execution — AI tasks check the Knowledge Base before calling the AI, using existing articles when a good match is found

At Enterprise scale, these optimizations can reduce AI costs by up to 91%.

KPI Dashboard

The top of the Incidents page shows key metrics: total incidents, open count, investigating count, resolved count, critical incidents, and Mean Time to Resolution (MTTR).

Running AI Tasks

  1. Find a queued task in the task list

Troubleshooting

My probe isn't connecting

Ensure the probe agent script is running on the target server, and that outbound HTTPS traffic on port 443 is allowed. Check the probe status in Configure — it should show "Heartbeat" with a recent timestamp.

AI agents aren't generating responses

Check that you have at least one AI provider configured in Service Metrics → AI Providers. Verify the API key is valid by using the "Test" button. Also check that you have AI Shadow enabled on your role subscriptions.

I'm getting "Please wait a moment" messages in Agent Chat

This is the built-in rate limiter preventing duplicate API calls. Wait 5 seconds between messages. This protects your AI budget from accidental rapid-fire requests.

Incident/Change won't transition to the next status

ITIL lifecycle transitions follow a specific order. For example, you can't move an incident from "Open" directly to "Resolved" — it must go through "Investigating" and "In Progress" first. Check the lifecycle pipeline diagram for the correct order.

No assets are appearing after adding a probe

After deploying a probe, you need to run a discovery scan. Go to Infrastructure → Discovery, select your probe, and trigger a scan. Make sure the credentials you've assigned are correct for the devices on your network.

The page shows "Showing 0 of 0" with no data

Make sure you're logged in with the correct account. All data is scoped to your user. If you're on a fresh account, you may need to go through onboarding and set up probes first to generate data.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between a Crew and an Agent?

A Crew is a department (e.g., Security Operations, Network Operations). An Agent is a specific role within that department (e.g., Security Monitor, Network Engineer). Think of Crews as teams and Agents as the roles on those teams.

Do AI agents replace my human team?

No. AI agents are designed to work alongside your human team. They handle routine monitoring, generate alerts, and propose actions — but critical decisions always require human approval. The platform follows a "human-first" philosophy.

How much does AI usage cost?

AI costs depend on usage volume. Use the AI Cost Estimator (Service Metrics tab) to see projected costs. HOLOCRON AI includes template caching and rate limiting that can reduce costs by up to 91% compared to raw API usage.

What is RTO and RPO?

RTO (Recovery Time Objective) is the maximum acceptable time to restore a service after a disruption. RPO (Recovery Point Objective) is the maximum acceptable amount of data loss measured in time. For example, an RPO of 1 hour means you can afford to lose up to 1 hour of data.

Can I use my own AI provider?

Yes. Go to Service Metrics → AI Providers to add your own OpenAI, Google Gemini, Anthropic Claude, or any OpenAI-compatible provider. You can even assign different providers to different agent roles.

What types of probes can I deploy?

Three types: Coupled (always connected, real-time), Semi-Autonomous (works offline, syncs periodically), and Fully Autonomous (self-contained, can self-remediate). Coupled is best for reliable networks, Autonomous is best for remote or unreliable locations.

How do I get the best results from Agent Chat?

Be specific in your questions. For infrastructure queries, mention the device name or type. For dashboards, ask for a "dashboard" or "overview" explicitly. For general knowledge, just ask naturally — the AI will switch to Strategic Advisor mode automatically.

What ITIL processes does HOLOCRON AI support?

Incident Management, Problem Management, Change Management, Service Request Management, Knowledge Management, Business Continuity Management (BCP/DRP), and Service Level Management. All follow standard ITIL lifecycle patterns with validated status transitions.
